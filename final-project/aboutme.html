Yoli's Horror Picks
~About Me~

I grew up thinking that I was not a fan of horror movies. I watched Jim Henson's The Dark Crystal when I was a small child and I remember being terrified of the evil and ancient-bird like creatures called the Skekis, who were stealing the life forces of other creatures. I had nightmares when my babysitter allowed me to watch episodes of The Twilight Zone-- she was subsequently fired. I remembered one episode where a person sewed her mouth shut after hearing voices from the food in her apartment. They were her friends and she wanted to spare their life. After the woman sewed her mouth shut, she starved to death.

Those images stuck in my mind and my mom realized that I was not ready for horror. I had many nightmares that woke me up at night, and required soothing from my mom. Several years later, when I had had a dose of real life and the terrifying things that can happen in reality. I thought I'd give horror a try again. Beetlejuice was one of my favorites, a horror comedy with Lydia Deetz, a character I could relate to. The Nightmare Before Christmas, was another palatable horror movie. The Blade movies with Wesley Snipes was groundbreaking for me. I got really into vampire movies. I'm sure that teens getting interested in monsters is a common thing. I enjoyed reading Frankenstein in high school. As an adult, I mostly did call myself a fan of horror because I'm 90% scaredy cat. But having worked in a library for 11 years, I have realized that I actually do have a lot of recommendations. Particularly, horror-comedy and gothic horror. My favorite stories are about monsters, and the complexity of humanity.
